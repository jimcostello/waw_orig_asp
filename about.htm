Staff
Writing at Work benefits from a network of writers, trainers, software specialists, and graphic artists. All have advanced professional degrees and in-depth business experience.

   









   

Steven Schultz, Ph.D., is president of Writing at Work. Since starting Writing at Work in 1985, he has trained business and industry professionals to produce their own effective and clear documentation.

He has also written, edited, and managed large-scale communications projects. These projects include web sites, marketing materials, self-study and training guides, standard operating procedures, technical reports, computer user manuals, educational textbooks, and articles for professional publication. He has designed and taught electronic distance-learning, CD-based, web-based, and on-line college courses.

Steven has taught at several universities and colleges in their graduate schools of business, corporate training divisions, and English departments. Steven graduated Phi Beta Kappa and Department's Distinction from the University of Illinois, with a Class Merit Scholar Award for his year's study at the University of Edinburgh, Scotland. His doctorate in English is from the State University of New York at Buffalo.

Robert Steffeck, senior writer, has worked for 20 years as a research chemist in new product development and pharmaceutical R & D. Well immersed in the language of science and engineering, he is an active copywriter and editor and a valuable resource for scientific marketing projects.

Robert has written marketing literature for Alltech Associates (Grace Davison), Resolution Systems, and SIELC Technologies. These projects included converting technical information to marketing literature, including website content, brochures, catalogs, and posters.

Robert has authored publications in chiral and achiral separations, ion-exclusion chromatography, and gas chromatography. While at Alltech, he published the first extensive comparison of C18 and C8 columns. At Pfizer, he published a new mode of chromatography, enantioselective ion-exclusion, and published the first separation of a secondary amine on a chiral crown ether in the then 35-year history of crown ethers.

Jim Costello, Ph.D., senior writer, brings strong writing talents, programming skills, and educational expertise to product development and service delivery. He specializes in creative and effective uses of the Internet for training, marketing, documentation, and support. For the past 20 years, Jim has worked in both the private and public sectors as an editor, writer, trainer, instructional designer, computer-based training developer, and web designer.

He is an Oracle Certified Application Developer and a member of ASP Guild International and the American Society for Training & Development. He was a presenter at Multimedia 96 Conference and Trade Show and a three-time presenter at SALT's Annual Conference on Interactive Systems for Training, Education, and Job Performance.

Jim has edited and written a variety of publications from advanced engineering reference books to primary school textbooks to computer user manuals to annual reports. He has written and published several articles of his own as well as a full-length book on baseball, In the Shadows of the Diamond. Jim's Ph.D. in English and American Literature is from the State University of New York at Buffalo.

Mark Malcolm, M.A., manages large project engagements for Writing at Work. He has authored and edited several management and organizational manuals, such as employee policy manuals, newsletters, and procedures guides. His technical writing projects include product and installation guides.

Mark also manages document production and quality control, making sure projects meet scheduling and budget expectations. Mark received his B. A. from the University of Chicago and his M. A. in Industrial Relations from the University of Minnesota.
